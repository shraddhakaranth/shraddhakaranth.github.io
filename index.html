About Me

I am a passionate scientist, with expertise in food microbiology, microbial risk assessment, food quality and safety assessment, immunobiology, and machine learning and statistical analysis. I received my Ph.D. in Food Science from the University of Maryland, College Park, and have completed 2.5 years of post-doctoral research in the same field. Over the span of my research career, I have published 13 journal articles and 2 book chapters with 540 total citations, collaborated with researchers from multiple fields, and presented my research in several international conferences and symposia. I specialize in using cross-disciplinary approaches to solving current prevalent issues in the field of food science. As an example, I have used novel statistical methods and machine learning to analyze microbial whole genome sequencing data to employ in microbial risk assessment. My research has contributed significantly to bringing microbial risk assessments into the genomics and machine learning era, earning me multiple academic awards and netting my lab ~$700K in research funding. I am dedicated to advancing food safety through innovative data-driven approaches and have mentored numerous students throughout my academic journey.
